Patrice Lumumba (1925–1961) was one of the key figures in the Democratic Republic of Congo’s struggle for independence. As the leader of the Congolese nationalist movement and founder of the MNC, he became the country’s first Prime Minister in 1960. A strong advocate for national unity and true political and economic sovereignty, he soon faced internal conflicts and foreign interference. Removed from power just months after independence, he was assassinated in 1961. Today, Lumumba is widely remembered as a symbol of the fight for freedom, justice, and decolonization in Africa.His vision continues to inspire generations of leaders and activists across Africa.

News

The Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) is a country rich in natural resources and cultural diversity. However, some regions in the east of the country are still experiencing armed conflicts. Among the active rebel groups, the M23 is one of the most well-known, causing tensions and population displacement. Despite this, the country continues to develop and work toward peace and stability, while attracting international attention to security and development in the region.
