Iconic monuments

A massive fortification stretching over 21,000 km, originally built to defend China from northern invasions. It crosses mountains, deserts, and valleys, making it one of the most iconic structures in the world. Today, it stands as a symbol of China’s long and complex history.


Great Wall of China (长城)

The former imperial palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties, located in the heart of Beijing. Its vast complex of red and gold buildings was once accessible only to the emperor and his court. Now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is a cultural landmark of immense importance.


Forbidden City (紫禁城)

A monumental collection of thousands of clay soldiers buried with the first emperor, Qin Shi Huang (秦始皇). Each statue has a unique face and posture, handcrafted with remarkable detail. It is considered one of the greatest archaeological discoveries of the 20th century.
