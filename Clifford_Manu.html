Palestine - The Holy Land

The Palestinian flag represents the rich heritage and resilience of the Palestinian people.

Palestine at a Glance

Capital Jerusalem (Al-Quds)
Official Language Arabic
Major Religions Islam, Christianity, Judaism
Currency Israeli Shekel (ILS), Jordanian Dinar (JOD)
Heritage Status Contains some of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities

Best Places to Feature

  • Dome of the Rock in Jerusalem - Iconic Islamic shrine
  • Church of the Nativity in Bethlehem - Birthplace of Jesus Christ
  • The Dead Sea - Lowest point on Earth with therapeutic minerals
  • Old City of Hebron - Ancient city with rich history
  • Mediterranean beaches in Gaza - Beautiful coastal landscapes

Cultural & Historical Heritage

Religious Significance

Palestine is home to three major religions - Islam, Christianity, and Judaism, making it one of the most spiritually significant regions in the world.

Ancient Cities

Contains some of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, including Jericho, one of the oldest inhabited cities globally.

Archaeological Wealth

Rich archaeological heritage with countless historical sites dating back thousands of years, telling the story of human civilization.

Traditional Embroidery

Traditional Palestinian embroidery patterns (Tatriz) feature intricate designs that represent different regions and tell cultural stories through fabric art.
